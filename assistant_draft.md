My name is Morgan Swann. I currently work in home improvement, but my professional roots run deep in the beverage alcohol industry — spanning more than five years of experience both on and off premise. My connection to hospitality stretches back to 2014, and in 2024 I became WSET Level 2 certified in wine. Wine, spirits, and beer aren't just categories I've worked in — they're passions I carry with me every day.

## The Problem

The beverage alcohol industry is facing a quiet crisis. Consumers are overwhelmed by the sheer volume of available products and, without the knowledge to navigate them confidently, many are simply opting out. Alcohol consumption has declined sharply — dropping from 67% in 2022 to 54% in 2025 — driven by growing health concerns and a persistent lack of product education among everyday shoppers. The industry risks losing not just sales, but entire generations of potential consumers who never found their entry point.[^1]

[^1]: Ansira, "2026 Alcohol Trends: What to Expect in the Coming Year," January 2026, https://ansira.com/blog/2026-alcohol-trends/

## How AI Could Help

- **Personalized product discovery and recommendations** tiered across good, better, and best options — meeting consumers at their budget and taste preferences without requiring them to already know what they like
- **Food pairing suggestions and cocktail/mocktail recipes** tailored to what a consumer is already buying or browsing, turning a single bottle into an experience
- **Education on ratings and awards** — demystifying scores, medals, and critical acclaim so consumers can shop with confidence rather than confusion
